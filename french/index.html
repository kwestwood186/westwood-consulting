Recommended Resources

Daily habit

Duolingo is useful for short beginner practice and momentum. Keep it to 10 minutes and pair it with speaking aloud.

Adult structure

Babbel or a beginner CEFR course can fill in pronunciation, grammar, and conversation patterns more directly.

Immersion input

Rosetta Stone, library apps, French songs, picture books, and cartoons help your ear adjust without pressure.

Feedback

Alliance Française or a tutor is best when you want real-time correction, especially for pronunciation and confidence.

Program Principles

  • Short daily practice beats occasional long sessions.
  • Listening and speaking come before perfect grammar.
  • Active recall and spaced review make vocabulary stick.
  • French at home should feel warm, brief, and low-pressure.
  • English reading still helps literacy and school confidence.

How to Use This Site

What to do each time

Daily routine

  1. Open Today.
  2. Do the four session blocks: Lesson, Listen, Review, Child moment.
  3. Press Listen beside each French phrase and repeat out loud.
  4. Try the Child Moment once with your son. Keep it short and easy.
  5. Click Complete session when you are finished.
  6. Open Flashcards for a few minutes of review.

Use the tabs

Weekly rhythm

  • Monday to Friday: one daily session.
  • Weekend: repeat one useful day or review flashcards.
  • Anytime: search the Phrasebook when a school/home situation comes up.

First week

  • Do not try to master everything.
  • Use only one or two phrases with your son.
  • Say French out loud even if it feels awkward.
  • Let the site move you to the next day after completion.
